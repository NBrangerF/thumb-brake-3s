# Hook Taxonomy V0

This resource defines the Hook Library V0 taxonomy for 3-5 second commerce video openings.

## Primary Attention Mechanisms

| ID | Mechanism | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| H1 | Sensory Salience | Color, motion, sound, rhythm, or composition captures attention. |
| H2 | High-Arousal Stimulation | Danger, pain anticipation, disgust, conflict, taboo hint, or overload creates stop power. |
| H3 | Curiosity Gap | Suspense, counterintuitive claims, hidden causes, or unfinished actions create information demand. |
| H4 | Self-Relevance | The viewer recognizes their identity, mistake, pain point, or daily situation. |
| H5 | Outcome / Proof | The opening shows results, proof, comparison, or visible transformation first. |
| H6 | Social Cue | Face, gaze, testimonial, crowd reaction, or comment overlay directs attention. |
| H7 | Cultural Recognition | Meme grammar, genre openings, era style, platform grammar, or symbolic structures trigger recognition. |

## Cross Axes

- Product relation: direct, problem_first, conflict_first, emotion_first, symbol_first, pure_decoy_observation.
- Narrative function: incident_trigger, crisis_escalation, social_conflict, embarrassment_moment, forbidden_action, rescue_setup, transformation_setup.
- Emotional path: tension_to_relief, disgust_to_satisfaction, embarrassment_to_confidence, chaos_to_order, curiosity_to_reveal, conflict_to_resolution, desire_to_projection, shock_to_proof.
- Generation mode: first_frame, video_reference, hybrid.
- Stimulation level: S0-S4 as creative intensity, not compliance risk.
